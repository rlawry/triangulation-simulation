Kepler's Method of Triangulation

From Astronomia Nova, 1609

  • Sidereal Period of Mars: 687 Days.
  • - After 687 days, when Mars returns to its original position around the Sun.
  • Begin Observation: Click the button to draw a ray from Earth to Mars
  • - At this moment your experiment will begin. It's going to be a long experiment. After 687 days, the final observation will be made automatically.
  • - Where your two observations intersect is a place on the orbit of Mars.
  • Perform many observations! Once you have collected years upon years of observations, pause the experiment and click "Make Conclusion"
  • Conclude your experiment: Use the WASD keys on your keyboard or drag your screen to adjust the shape of your ellipse to make the orbit of Mars.
  • - Compare your results to known values. How close were you?
  • Heliocentric Longitude: The angle of Earth around the Sun
  • Geocentric Longitude: The angle of Mars around Earth
